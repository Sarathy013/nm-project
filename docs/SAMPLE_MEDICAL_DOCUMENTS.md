## Document 1: Pneumonia Clinical Guidelines

### Symptoms

Pneumonia typically presents with the following symptoms:
- Fever (often high-grade, >38°C)
- Productive cough with purulent sputum
- Pleuritic chest pain (worsens with deep breathing)
- Shortness of breath (dyspnea)
- Tachypnea (rapid breathing)
- Tachycardia (rapid heart rate)
- Fatigue and malaise
- Confusion (especially in elderly patients)

In atypical pneumonia, symptoms may be more gradual and include dry cough, headache, and myalgia.

### Diagnosis

Diagnostic criteria for pneumonia include:
1. Clinical presentation consistent with lower respiratory tract infection
2. New infiltrate on chest radiograph (gold standard)
3. Supportive findings: elevated WBC count, hypoxemia

Differential diagnosis includes:
- Acute bronchitis (no infiltrate on X-ray)
- Pulmonary embolism
- Heart failure
- Lung malignancy
- Tuberculosis

### Treatment

**Outpatient Treatment (mild-moderate cases):**
- First-line: Amoxicillin 1g TID for 5-7 days
- Alternative: Doxycycline 100mg BID for 5-7 days
- For comorbidities: Amoxicillin-clavulanate PLUS macrolide (azithromycin)

**Inpatient Treatment (severe cases):**
- Beta-lactam (ceftriaxone, ampicillin-sulbactam) PLUS macrolide
- Respiratory fluoroquinolone monotherapy (levofloxacin, moxifloxacin)

**Supportive Care:**
- Oxygen therapy for hypoxemia (SpO2 < 90%)
- IV fluids for dehydration
- Antipyretics for fever
- Analgesics for pleuritic pain

### Guidelines

**CURB-65 Score for Severity Assessment:**
- **C**onfusion (new onset)
- **U**rea > 19 mg/dL (7 mmol/L)
- **R**espiratory rate ≥ 30/min
- **B**lood pressure (systolic < 90 or diastolic ≤ 60)
- **65** years or older

Score interpretation:
- 0-1: Outpatient treatment appropriate
- 2: Consider hospitalization
- ≥3: Severe pneumonia, ICU consideration

**Prevention:**
- Pneumococcal vaccination (PCV13, PPSV23)
- Annual influenza vaccination
- Smoking cessation
- Hand hygiene

## Document 2: Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

### Symptoms

Classic symptoms of hyperglycemia:
- Polyuria (frequent urination)
- Polydipsia (increased thirst)
- Polyphagia (increased hunger)
- Unexplained weight loss
- Fatigue and weakness
- Blurred vision
- Slow-healing wounds
- Recurrent infections (UTIs, skin, yeast)
- Acanthosis nigricans (darkened skin patches)

Many patients are asymptomatic and diagnosed through screening.

### Diagnosis

Diagnostic criteria (any one confirmed on two separate occasions):
1. Fasting plasma glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L)
2. HbA1c ≥ 6.5%
3. 2-hour plasma glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL during OGTT
4. Random plasma glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL with classic symptoms

**Prediabetes:**
- Fasting glucose 100-125 mg/dL
- HbA1c 5.7-6.4%

### Treatment

**Lifestyle Modifications (foundation of therapy):**
- Medical nutrition therapy (carbohydrate counting, portion control)
- Physical activity: ≥150 min/week moderate exercise
- Weight loss: 5-10% of body weight
- Diabetes self-management education

**Pharmacologic Therapy:**

First-line: **Metformin** 500-2000 mg/day
- Contraindicated in eGFR < 30
- Hold before contrast procedures

Second-line options (based on comorbidities):
- **ASCVD**: GLP-1 RA (semaglutide, liraglutide) or SGLT2i
- **Heart Failure**: SGLT2i (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin)
- **CKD**: SGLT2i or GLP-1 RA
- **Weight loss priority**: GLP-1 RA

Other agents:
- Sulfonylureas (glipizide, glimepiride) - risk of hypoglycemia
- DPP-4 inhibitors (sitagliptin, linagliptin) - weight neutral
- TZDs (pioglitazone) - avoid in heart failure
- Insulin - for severe hyperglycemia or symptomatic patients

### Guidelines

**Glycemic Targets:**
- HbA1c: < 7.0% for most adults
- Fasting glucose: 80-130 mg/dL
- Postprandial glucose: < 180 mg/dL

**Screening Recommendations:**
- All adults ≥ 35 years
- Earlier if overweight (BMI ≥ 25) + risk factors
- Repeat every 3 years if normal

**Complications Screening:**
- Annual dilated eye exam
- Annual urine albumin/creatinine ratio
- Comprehensive foot exam at each visit
- Blood pressure monitoring at each visit

## Document 3: Hypertension Management

### Symptoms

Hypertension is typically **asymptomatic** ("silent killer").

When severe (hypertensive crisis), may present with:
- Severe headache
- Chest pain
- Shortness of breath
- Visual changes
- Confusion
- Nausea/vomiting
- Seizures (hypertensive encephalopathy)

### Diagnosis

**Blood Pressure Categories (ACC/AHA 2017):**

| Category | Systolic | Diastolic |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Normal | < 120 | AND < 80 |
| Elevated | 120-129 | AND < 80 |
| Stage 1 HTN | 130-139 | OR 80-89 |
| Stage 2 HTN | ≥ 140 | OR ≥ 90 |

Diagnosis requires:
- Average of ≥ 2 readings on ≥ 2 separate occasions
- Proper technique: seated, rested 5 min, correct cuff size
- Consider white-coat and masked hypertension

### Treatment

**Lifestyle Modifications (all patients):**
- DASH diet (fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy, reduced saturated fat)
- Sodium reduction: < 2300 mg/day (ideally < 1500 mg)
- Weight loss: 1 kg loss ≈ 1 mmHg BP reduction
- Physical activity: 90-150 min/week aerobic + resistance
- Alcohol moderation: men ≤ 2, women ≤ 1 drinks/day
- Smoking cessation

**Pharmacologic Therapy:**

**First-line agents:**
- Thiazide diuretics (chlorthalidone, HCTZ)
- ACE inhibitors (lisinopril, enalapril)
- ARBs (losartan, valsartan)
- Calcium channel blockers (amlodipine, nifedipine)

**Special populations:**
- **Black patients**: Thiazide or CCB preferred first-line
- **CKD**: ACEi or ARB (renoprotective)
- **Diabetes**: ACEi or ARB first-line
- **Pregnancy**: Methyldopa, labetalol, nifedipine (avoid ACEi/ARB)

**Resistant Hypertension (uncontrolled on 3 meds):**
- Add spironolactone (if eGFR > 45, K+ < 4.5)
- Evaluate for secondary causes

### Guidelines

**Treatment Thresholds:**
- **Stage 1 HTN + ASCVD risk ≥ 10%**: Start medication
- **Stage 1 HTN + ASCVD risk < 10%**: Lifestyle, reassess 3-6 months
- **Stage 2 HTN**: Start medication immediately (often 2 agents)

**Blood Pressure Targets:**
- Most adults: < 130/80 mmHg
- Elderly/frail: Individualize (may accept < 140/90)

**Follow-up:**
- Monthly until controlled
- Then every 3-6 months

## Document 4: Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)

### Symptoms

**Classic Presentation:**
- Chest pain/pressure (substernal, radiating to arm, jaw, back)
- Described as "squeezing," "tightness," "heavy"
- Associated symptoms: dyspnea, diaphoresis, nausea, lightheadedness
- Duration: > 20 minutes (distinguishes from stable angina)

**Atypical Presentations (more common in women, elderly, diabetics):**
- Epigastric pain (may mimic indigestion)
- Isolated dyspnea
- Syncope
- Confusion
- Fatigue without chest pain

### Diagnosis

**Initial Workup (within 10 minutes of presentation):**
1. 12-lead ECG (look for ST elevation, depression, T-wave inversion)
2. Cardiac biomarkers (troponin I/T - serial measurements)
3. Chest X-ray
4. Basic labs (CBC, BMP, coagulation panel, lipids)

**ECG Classification:**
- **STEMI**: ST elevation in contiguous leads (requires immediate reperfusion)
- **NSTEMI**: No ST elevation, elevated troponin
- **Unstable Angina**: No ST elevation, normal troponin

**Risk Stratification:**
- TIMI Score
- GRACE Score
- HEART Score

### Treatment

**Immediate Management (MONA-B):**
- **M**orphine (for pain not relieved by nitrates)
- **O**xygen (if SpO2 < 90% or respiratory distress)
- **N**itroglycerin (sublingual, then IV if needed; avoid if RV infarct or PDE5 inhibitor use)
- **A**spirin 325 mg chewed (unless contraindicated)
- **B**eta-blocker (within 24 hours if no contraindications)

**Antiplatelet Therapy:**
- Dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT): Aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor
- P2Y12 options: Clopidogrel, Ticagrelor, Prasugrel

**Anticoagulation:**
- Unfractionated heparin or LMWH (enoxaparin)
- Fondaparinux as alternative

**Reperfusion (STEMI only):**
- **Primary PCI**: Preferred, within 90 minutes of first medical contact
- **Fibrinolysis**: If PCI unavailable within 120 minutes (alteplase, tenecteplase)

**Long-term Management:**
- High-intensity statin (atorvastatin 80mg or rosuvastatin 20-40mg)
- ACE inhibitor (especially if LVEF < 40%, diabetes, CKD)
- Beta-blocker (for 3 years minimum, indefinitely if LVEF < 40%)
- Cardiac rehabilitation

### Guidelines

**Secondary Prevention:**
- LDL target: < 70 mg/dL (or < 55 mg/dL for very high risk)
- BP target: < 130/80 mmHg
- Smoking cessation (absolute priority)
- Cardiac rehab enrollment (Class I recommendation)
- Influenza vaccination

## Document 5: Asthma Management

### Symptoms

Classic asthma symptoms:
- Wheezing (expiratory, may be inspiratory in severe cases)
- Dyspnea (often episodic)
- Chest tightness
- Cough (worse at night or early morning)
- Symptoms triggered by: allergens, exercise, cold air, viral infections, irritants

Symptoms are typically **variable** and **reversible**.

### Diagnosis

**Clinical Criteria:**
1. Recurrent episodes of wheezing, coughing, dyspnea, chest tightness
2. Variable expiratory airflow limitation (demonstrated by one of):
   - Bronchodilator reversibility: FEV1 increases > 12% AND > 200mL
   - Positive bronchoprovocation test
   - Peak flow variability > 10% (children) or > 20% (adults)

**Spirometry Findings:**
- Reduced FEV1/FVC ratio (< 0.75-0.80 in adults)
- Improvement post-bronchodilator

**Severity Classification (before treatment):**
- **Intermittent**: Symptoms ≤ 2 days/week, nighttime ≤ 2x/month
- **Mild Persistent**: Symptoms > 2 days/week, nighttime 3-4x/month
- **Moderate Persistent**: Daily symptoms, nighttime > 1x/week
- **Severe Persistent**: Symptoms throughout day, nighttime 4-7x/week

### Treatment

**Quick-Relief Medications (Rescue):**
- SABA: Albuterol, Levalbuterol (first-line for acute symptoms)
- SAMA: Ipratropium (adjunct in severe exacerbations)
- Systemic corticosteroids: Prednisone, Methylprednisolone (for exacerbations)

**Long-Term Control Medications:**

**Step 1 (Intermittent):** SABA PRN

**Step 2 (Mild Persistent):**
- Low-dose ICS (Fluticasone, Budesonide, Beclomethasone)
- Alternative: LTRA (Montelukast)

**Step 3 (Moderate Persistent):**
- Low-dose ICS + LABA (combination inhaler)
- OR Medium-dose ICS

**Step 4-5 (Severe Persistent):**
- Medium/High-dose ICS + LABA
- Consider biologics: Omalizumab (anti-IgE), Mepolizumab (anti-IL5)

**Asthma Action Plan:**
- Green zone: Well controlled (continue maintenance)
- Yellow zone: Caution (increase rescue, may need steroids)
- Red zone: Medical alert (seek emergency care)

### Guidelines

**GINA 2023 Recommendations:**
- All adults/adolescents should receive ICS-containing therapy
- SABA-only treatment no longer recommended
- Consider SMART therapy (Single Maintenance And Reliever Therapy) with ICS-formoterol

**Exacerbation Management:**
- SABA + Ipratropium via nebulizer
- Systemic corticosteroids (Prednisone 40-60mg or equivalent)
- Oxygen to maintain SpO2 ≥ 90%
- Consider IV Magnesium sulfate for severe cases
